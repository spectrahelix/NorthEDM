## Goods and Services Descriptions (by Class)

### Class 35 — Advertising and Business Services
Online marketplace services connecting festival and event vendors with consumers for the purpose of placing and fulfilling delivery orders; vendor enrollment and management services for festival and event operators; business administration software services for managing delivery orders at live events and festivals.

### Class 39 — Transportation and Delivery Services
On-demand delivery services for goods ordered at festivals, outdoor events, and live music events; courier and delivery services operating within festival grounds and event venues; coordination of delivery personnel for the transport of goods from vendor locations to customer campsites and designated locations within event grounds.

### Class 42 — Technology and Software Services
Software as a service (SaaS) providing a platform for ordering, accepting, tracking, and managing delivery orders at live events and festivals; providing a mobile-optimized web application for vendors to receive and manage delivery orders in real time; providing consumers with an online ordering platform that allows submission of location photographs and delivery window preferences for festival-based delivery.

---

## Description of Use

The mark FESTDASH is used in commerce in connection with an online and mobile platform that enables attendees of festivals and live events to order goods from participating vendors and receive delivery directly to their campsite or designated location within the event grounds. The service allows customers to specify their campsite zone, upload photographs of their location, and designate a preferred delivery window. Vendors participating in the FestDash network receive real-time order notifications on a tablet or mobile device and may accept or decline orders. Designated delivery personnel fulfill accepted orders on behalf of the vendor.

## Notes for Your Attorney

1. Conduct a full clearance search on FESTDASH and similar marks (FESTDASH, FEST DASH, FESTIVAL DASH) before filing to confirm no conflicts.
2. Consider filing in Class 43 (food service / hospitality) if the platform expands to food delivery.
3. Evaluate whether the design logo (if one is developed) warrants a separate design mark application.
4. If operating only in the US initially, file with the USPTO. If international expansion is planned, consider a Madrid Protocol application.
5. A federal trademark gives nationwide priority from the filing date — file sooner rather than later, especially before public launch.
